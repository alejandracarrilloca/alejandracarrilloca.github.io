Orientation Syntax of Insulator-Associated Motifs in Drosophila

Stowers Institute for Medical Research Summer Scholars Program

Poster Presentation

Kansas City, Missouri, USA · July 2026

This presentation summarizes a computational analysis of motif orientation within Drosophila insulator islands. Using permutation-based statistical models, the project tests whether local and island-wide orientation patterns occur more frequently than expected by chance and identifies specific motif-pair interactions that contribute to the observed organization.

Exploring the Role of SPN-205: A Nonsense-Mediated Decay-Targeted CD43 Isoform

XXVI National Congress of the Mexican Society for Immunology

Oral and Poster

Chihuahua, Mexico · October 2025

This presentation examines SPN-205, a predicted nonsense-mediated decay-targeted isoform of the SPN gene. Using transcriptomic data from The Cancer Genome Atlas, the study evaluates its expression across tumor types and explores whether SPN-205 may have previously uncharacterized roles in cancer biology and immune regulation.
