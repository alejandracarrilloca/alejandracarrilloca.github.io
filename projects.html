Targeted Disruption of CD43 Using CRISPR-Cas9 Genome Editing

Institute of Biotechnology, National Autonomous University of Mexico

Ongoing

This project uses CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing to disrupt SPN, the gene encoding the immune-cell surface protein CD43. By generating CD43-knockout cell lines, we aim to determine how the loss of CD43 affects cellular responses to extracellular signals and its broader role in immune regulation.

Characterizing the functional consequences of CD43 loss may provide insight into its contribution to immune-cell behavior and cancer progression, particularly in hematological malignancies.

Research question

How does CD43 loss alter immune-cell signaling, behavior, and regulatory functions?

Approach

CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing, cell-line generation, validation, and functional characterization.

Biological context

Immune regulation, CD43 biology, and hematological malignancies.

Exploring the Role of SPN-205: A Nonsense-Mediated Decay-Targeted CD43 Isoform

Institute of Biotechnology, National Autonomous University of Mexico

Ongoing

Dulce Alejandra Carrillo Carlos, Luis Eduardo Chipres Naranjo, and Yvonne Jane Rosenstein Azoulay

This project investigates SPN-205, a predicted nonsense-mediated decay-targeted isoform of SPN. Nonsense-mediated decay is an RNA quality-control pathway that removes transcripts containing premature termination codons. Dysregulation of this pathway in cancer can stabilize aberrant transcripts and remodel the transcriptome.

Using publicly available transcriptomic datasets from The Cancer Genome Atlas, we compared the expression of canonical SPN and SPN-205 between tumor and normal samples. SPN-205 was elevated in several tumor types, including acute myeloid leukemia, thymoma, testicular germ cell tumors, diffuse large B-cell lymphoma, pancreatic adenocarcinoma, and stomach adenocarcinoma.

Our results suggest that SPN-205 may represent more than transcriptional noise and could have previously uncharacterized functions or interactions in cancer. Ongoing analyses are evaluating the relationship between SPN-205 expression and signatures associated with proliferation, immune evasion, and resistance to apoptosis.

Research question

Is SPN-205 differentially expressed in cancer, and could it contribute to cancer-associated molecular processes?

Approach

TCGA transcriptomic analysis

Biological context

Alternative isoforms, nonsense-mediated decay, immune regulation, and cancer biology.
