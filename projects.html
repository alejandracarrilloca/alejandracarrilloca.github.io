“Exploring the role of SPN-205: an nonsense-mediated decay targeted CD43 isoform in cancer”

Dulce Alejandra Carrillo Carlos¹, Luis Eduardo Chipres Naranjo¹, Yvonne Jane Rosenstein Azoulay¹
¹Institute of Biotechnology, National Autonomous University of Mexico

Status: 2025 - Ongoing

Transcript isoform characterization and construction of detailed transcript maps for all SPN isoforms, identifying non-coding and NMD-targeted variants.

Computational analysis of TCGA and GTEx data to evaluate the expression of SPN-205, a predicted NMD targeted isoform of SPN. We found SPN-205 overexpressed in multiple tumor types, suggesting a potential functional role beyond transcriptional noise. Our ongoing work includes functional analysis to assess the correlation between the expression of SPN and SPN-205 with gene expression signatures associated with cell proliferation, immune evasion, and apoptosis resistance.

“Targeted Disruption of CD43 Using CRISPR-Cas9 Genome Editing”

Dulce Alejandra Carrillo Carlos¹, Luis Eduardo Chipres Naranjo¹, Yvonne Jane Rosenstein Azoulay¹
¹Institute of Biotechnology, National Autonomous University of Mexico

Status: 2024 - Ongoing

This project employs CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing to specifically disrupt the SPN gene, responsible for producing the CD43 protein found on immune cells. By generating CD43 knockout cell lines, we aim to investigate how the absence of this protein impacts various immune cell functions, including their ability to respond to external signals and participate in immune regulation. Understanding the functional consequences of CD43 loss will provide insights into its role in immune system dynamics and its contribution to cancer progression, particularly in hematological malignancies.
